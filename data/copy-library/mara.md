## Shared Mara Context

All three properties share the same river, the same ecosystem, and the same location at the heart of the Maasai Mara's Olare Orok concession. Copy that references the land applies across all three. Copy that references the camp experience is property-specific.

**Shared reference points:**
- The Olare Orok River — the defining physical feature. All three camps face it.
- Big Cat Territory — Olare Orok is known for high lion, leopard, and cheetah density
- Near Olkiombo airstrip — relevant for logistics/factsheets
- The Great Wildebeest Migration — seasonal, but a major draw for Luxury Migration Camp especially

### Soroi Mara Bush Camp

**Configuration:** 12 luxury eco-conscious tents (river-facing) + Bush Wing (4 simpler tents) + 2 family tents
**Setting:** Immersed in tree foliage — the camp disappears into the vegetation
**Positioning:** Eco-conscious. The most naturalistic of the three Mara properties.
**Copy angle:** The Mara as living ecosystem, not spectacular backdrop. The trees matter. The river matters. The eco-credential is understated, not shouted.
