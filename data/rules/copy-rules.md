# Soroi Collection — Copy Rules & Tone of Voice

## Brand Character

The Soroi Collection is a Kenyan-owned and operated portfolio of luxury safari and coastal properties. The brand voice is warm, refined, and deeply rooted in place. It never reads like a generic luxury hotel brand. Kenya is not a backdrop — it is the substance.

Conservation and community values are genuine brand pillars, not marketing add-ons. They should come through as matter-of-fact conviction, not performative messaging.

---

## Core Tone Principles

**Warm but not casual.** Guests are welcomed as if into a family home — but it is a home with exceptional taste and standards.

**Refined but not cold.** Luxury is expressed through detail, knowledge, and care. Not through distance or formality.

**Specific, never generic.** "The Olare Orok River at dusk" beats "stunning sunsets." "Named after explorer Eric Larsen" beats "rich heritage." Specificity signals authenticity.

**Kenyan, always.** Use Kenyan place names, Swahili words where natural, references to local culture, wildlife, and landscape. Never write for a non-African audience as though Kenya needs explaining.

**Conservation as conviction.** Reference eco-credentials, community partnerships, and conservation values as facts — not selling points. The reader infers the values; don't state them as claims.

## Copy Rules by Format

### Headlines
- Short, evocative, grounded in place or experience
- Avoid superlatives ("the most," "the best," "unparalleled")
- Avoid generic luxury clichés ("escape," "discover," "unwind," "retreat")
- Prefer active, present-tense framing
- OK: "Where the Olare Orok meets the tree line." / "Ten tents. One river. The Mara."
- Avoid: "Discover Unparalleled Luxury in the Heart of Africa."

### Body Copy
- Short paragraphs — two to four sentences maximum
- One idea per paragraph
- Write for someone who has already chosen luxury — don't sell the category, sell the specific
- Use em dashes and short, rhythmic sentences for impact
- Avoid passive voice
- Avoid filler openers: "Nestled in…", "Set against…", "Welcome to…"

### Calls to Action (CTAs)
- Direct and specific: "Book your Mara dates", "Request the rate card", "Reserve Oldonyo"
- Avoid vague CTAs: "Find out more", "Learn more", "Explore now"
- Trade/agent CTAs may be softer: "Reach out to discuss rates" is fine for WhatsApp

### Social Captions (Instagram / Facebook)
- Lead with the image's strongest detail — don't describe what the reader can already see
- First line is the hook — it should work without the rest of the caption
- Keep captions under 150 words for Instagram; Facebook allows slightly more
- Use line breaks liberally for readability
- Hashtags go at the end, on a new line, separated from the caption
- Emoji use: minimal and purposeful — one or two max, never decorative
- Tag @soroicollection where appropriate

### WhatsApp (Trade Agent Messages)
- Conversational but professional — this is B2B, not consumer
- Short — three to five lines maximum. Agents read on mobile.
- Lead with the specific offer or update, not a greeting
- Include: property name, dates, rate, what's included, how to book
- OK to be direct and practical: "Hi [Agent], quick update on Larsens rates for Aug–Oct…"
- Avoid marketing language — agents want facts

### Factsheet Copy
- Objective and informative — the voice here is confident and descriptive, not promotional
- Use consistent section headers across properties for comparability
- Room/tent specifications: accurate numbers, configuration, and facing direction matter
- Amenities listed as bullets — no full sentences needed
- Include conservation/eco credentials where certified (e.g. Gold Eco-Rating for Larsens)

### Menu Copy
- Diani menus: light, coastal, evocative. Ingredient-led descriptions. Short.
- Safari properties: classic but unfussy. Names, key ingredients, provenance where relevant.
- Cocktail names should feel specific to place — Swahili words, landscape references, local botanicals
- Avoid pretentious or over-written food descriptions

## Tone by Property Tier

### Premium Tier — Private Wing, Larsens Camp
- Most restrained. Let silence do the work.
- Fewer words, higher density of meaning
- No offer language — speak to experience, not price

### Luxury Tier — Mara Bush Camp, Luxury Migration Camp, Lions Bluff, Soroi Blue Diani, Soroi Amboseli
- Warm, vivid, experiential
- Offer language is fine but secondary to experience
- Conservation and community references feel natural here

### Accessible Tier — Cheetah Tented Camp, Leopards Lair, Samburu Lodge
- More welcoming, slightly more informal
- Practical information is valued — include it without apology
- Samburu Lodge: corporate-capable tone for conference/group materials. Professional, clear, structured.

---

## Kenyan Context Notes

- Samburu, Mara, Amboseli, Tsavo, Diani: each has a distinct character — don't blend them
- Maasai cultural references appear most naturally for Mara and Amboseli properties
- Samburu people are distinct from Maasai — do not conflate
- Swahili words to use naturally: *asante*, *karibu*, *pole pole*, *safari*, *jambo* (sparingly — it skews touristic)
- Avoid "Africa" as a homogeneous noun when you mean Kenya specifically

---

## Things to Never Write

- "Nestled in the heart of Africa"
- "Untouched wilderness"
- "Once-in-a-lifetime experience"
- "World-class"
- "Unparalleled luxury"
- "The magic of Africa"
- "Where dreams come true"
- Anything that would appear in a generic travel brochure not specific to this collection
